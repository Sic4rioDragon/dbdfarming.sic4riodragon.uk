DBD Farming Guide

Asian Farming

The easier solo-friendly route when it works, but also the messier one compared to organized farming.

Watch the guide Compare with organized farming Back to home

Why people do it

  • It can be easier to solo queue into.
  • It is more relaxed when the lobby understands it.
  • You do not need to round up a full organized group first.

Main downsides

  • It is usually slower.
  • It can go wrong more easily.
  • Random players may not understand what is happening.
  • Some players may not want to participate at all.

Important caution

Asian farming can violate BHVR’s Terms of Use due to its unorganized nature and the risk of involving unwilling participants. It is also less reliable and can expose you to griefers or cheaters.

That caution is based on the simple idea that BHVR’s Terms of Use govern participation in the game and can be supplemented by posted rules for particular activities, so bringing random, uninformed players into a farming setup is the riskier side of this. That is an inference from the official terms, not a direct quoted line about “Asian farming.” :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

Common expectation

Bloody Party Streamers or anniversary cakes are not optional in practice. In many Asian farming situations, not bringing them can get you killed early, ignored, or treated as somebody who is not actually taking part properly.

Any Means Necessary is one of the perks commonly associated with this style, alongside the usual healing-focused perks.
