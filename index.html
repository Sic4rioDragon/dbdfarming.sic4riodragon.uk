What is DBD farming?

In Dead by Daylight, farming usually means playing matches in a way that helps players gain bloodpoints, experience, or grade progress more efficiently than standard matches.

The two main approaches most people talk about are Asian farming and organized farming. Out of the two, I want to especially highlight organized farming, because it is usually the most coordinated and efficient when everyone is on the same page.

Recommended

Organized Farming

The most efficient option when you have a full group.

  • All 5 players match together and coordinate.
  • Usually done in voice chat.
  • Allows more efficient builds and cleaner match flow.
  • Best option if your goal is serious BP/grade farming.
  • Needs a full group of people who actually get along.

I am mainly linking people to the group and their resources rather than reposting all of their detailed methods here.

Asian Farming

Easier to get into if you are solo queueing.

  • Easy to solo queue.
  • Very relaxed.
  • Usually slower matches.
  • Can occasionally go wrong.

This is the easier route for people who do not have a full organized group ready, but it is usually less efficient than organized farming.

Common things you usually need

Offerings

Bloody Party Streamers or anniversary cakes are commonly used.

Popular perks

Reactive Healing and Self-Care are very common. Any Means Necessary is commonly mentioned for Asian farming.

Coordination

Organized farming becomes much better when everybody knows the plan before the match starts.

Bloodpoint Farming

Bloodpoint farming is what most people mean when they search for DBD farming or Dead by Daylight bloodpoint farming.

The goal is to maximize BP gain through coordinated actions, the right offerings, efficient match flow, and builds that support repeated value during the match.

XP Farming

XP farming is a bit different from raw bloodpoint farming. People usually care about this when leveling account progress or just wanting consistent progress over many matches.

In practice, this often overlaps with farming setups, but the best-feeling method depends on how quickly matches can be finished while still giving useful progress.

Grade Farming

If your goal is killer grade progress, one strong method people talk about is using Pyramid Head and maintaining chase structure cleanly rather than just ending everything too fast.

A strong killer grade farming idea is: 5 M2s with survivors maintaining chase like Pyramid Head in basement, healing with Reactive Healing, Self-Care, Desperate Measures, and Leader for one survivor, then 2 Corrective Action while they maintain chase during heals, then down with M2 the final time and cage everyone.

I may expand this into a more detailed dedicated section later, but for now this gives the basic idea for people specifically searching for DBD killer grade farming.

Quick FAQ

Which one should I start with?

If you can get a full group together, start with organized farming. If you cannot, Asian farming is the easier solo-friendly route.

What is the main difference?

Organized farming is more coordinated and efficient. Asian farming is more relaxed and easier to try without a full setup.

What should I bring?

Usually bloodpoint offerings like Bloody Party Streamers or anniversary cakes, plus the commonly used healing/support perks mentioned above.
