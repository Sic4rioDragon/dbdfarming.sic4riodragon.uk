How does farming work?

Farming is basically just playing the match in a controlled way so everybody gets what they need out of it instead of playing it like a normal match.

Depending on the method, that can mean repeated heals, chases, downs, hooks, cages, resets, pallet actions, and other things that build points and grade progress efficiently.

What is the difference between organized and Asian farming?

Organized farming is planned. Everybody knows what is happening, usually everyone is in VC, and the methods are cleaner and faster.

Asian farming is more random. It is easier to solo queue into, but it is slower, less reliable, and can involve unwilling players.

How do I find a group?

Best place is the Bloodpoint Farming Discord. It is the biggest and most active farming community out there.

How can I change my region?

Usually people use a region changer for that. The most common one for this is Make Your Choice, usually shortened to MYC.

It is basically the best known option to use for this.

What offerings do I need?

Bloody Party Streamers or anniversary cakes are usually expected.

In Asian farming they are basically required. In organized farming other bloodpoint offerings can be accepted if it is communicated, but they are still usually unwanted.

What perks are commonly used?

The main ones you will see a lot are Reactive Healing and Self-Care.

For Asian farming, Any Means Necessary is also a big one. The fourth slot is often something like Leader, Prove Thyself, or Deja Vu.

How long does a round take?

Usually around 10 minutes. If done right, you generally max everything including XP.

Is organized farming the best option?

Yes, in my opinion it is the best option by far. It is cleaner, faster, easier to control, and everybody is actually on the same page.
